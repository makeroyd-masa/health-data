# South Korea Country Information — Travel requirements

- A visa is not required for stays 90 days or less for business or tourism.
- [The Korean Electronic Travel Authorization](https://www.k-eta.go.kr/portal/guide/viewetaapplication.do) (K-ETA) exemption for U.S. passport holders was extended through December 31, 2026. Starting January 1, 2027, you will need a K-ETA before traveling to South Korea.
- A visa is required for all other travel purposes, including employment, teaching English, and stays longer than 90 days.

- Not required

- Must be valid at the time of entry. There is no minimum expiration validity requirement.

- No declaration needed on entry or exit.
