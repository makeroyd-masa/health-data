# Iraq — IraqTraveler View

Iraq is reporting increased cases of [Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever](https://www.cdc.gov/crimean-congo-hemorrhagic/about/index.html) (CCHF). CCHF is spread through tick bites or the blood or body fluids of infected animals or people. To [prevent](https://www.cdc.gov/crimean-congo-hemorrhagic/about/index.html#:~:text=infections%20are%20common.-,Prevention,-To%20prevent%20CCHF) CCHF, travelers should use an [Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)-registered insect repellant](https://www.epa.gov/insect-repellents/find-repellent-right-you) to avoid tick bites. Travelers should also wear gloves, long sleeves, long pants, closed shoes, and socks when handling animals and avoid contact with the body fluids of potentially infected animals or people.
