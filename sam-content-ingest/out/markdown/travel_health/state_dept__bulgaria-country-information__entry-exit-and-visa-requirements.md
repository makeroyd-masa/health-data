#### Traveling with children

- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on [how to prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information/Bulgaria.html) in Bulgaria.
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html).

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

- The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of Bulgaria.

---

#### Currency

- The Euro is the official currency of Bulgaria.
- Credit cards are widely accepted but may not be accepted outside major cities.
- Damaged or worn U.S. dollar bank notes are rejected at banks or exchange bureaus.
- Most shops, hotels, and restaurants do not accept travelers’ checks.

---

#### Traveling through Europe

- If you are planning to visit or travel through Europe, be familiar with the requirements of the Schengen Borders Agreement. It allows you to move freely within several countries without border checks.
- Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months beyond your period of stay if you plan on transiting a Schengen country. You will need sufficient proof of funds and a return plane ticket.
- Review our information for [U.S. Travelers in Europe](/en/international-travel/planning/guidance/europe.html).
