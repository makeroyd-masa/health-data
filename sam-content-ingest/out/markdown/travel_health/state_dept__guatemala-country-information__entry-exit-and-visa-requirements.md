# Guatemala Country Information — Entry, exit, and visa requirements

#### Helpful resources

- [Embassy of Guatemala in the United States](https://www.embaguateusa.gob.gt/ "Embassy of Guatemala in the United States").
- Review our information on [Dual Nationality](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/dual-nationality.html "Dual Nationality").

---

#### Traveling with children

- Always bring a copy of each child’s birth certificate or other evidence of your legal relationship to each child. A notarized letter of consent to travel is recommended if a child is not traveling with both parents.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html "prevent an international parental child abduction"). You can also review [information on Parental Child Abduction](/en/international-parental-child-abduction.html "Parental Child Abduction") in Guatemala.
- Review information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html "Traveling with Minors").

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

- The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of Guatemala.
- Verify this information with the [Embassy of Guatemala](https://gt.usembassy.gov/ "Embassy of Guatemala") before you travel.

---

#### Currency

- The Guatemalan quetzal is the official currency of Guatemala.
- Credit cards are widely accepted in Guatemala City, Antigua, and other tourist destinations. However, be prepared to pay in cash in other areas or in smaller, local businesses.
- Guatemala has ATMs that accept most U.S.-issued bankcards, but they may charge high fees. ATMs are not widely available outside tourist areas.
