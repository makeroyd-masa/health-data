# Antarctica Country Information — Entry, exit, and visa requirements

#### Helpful resources

- [International Association of Antarctica Tour Operators](https://iaato.org/)

---

#### Passports and visas

You need a U.S. passport to travel through any country on your way to or from Antarctica. Please review the [destination information](/en/international-travel/travel-advisories.html) for those countries.

---

##### Expeditions to Antarctica

U.S. tourists planning a private trip to Antarctica must notify the Department of State by emailing [Antarctica@state.gov](mailto:Antarctica@state.gov). Do this at least 3 months before traveling to the Antarctic Treaty area.

---

#### Traveling with children

- Always bring a copy of each child’s birth certificate or other evidence of your legal relationship to each child.
- Parents and guardians should prepare a notarized, written permission letter. It should allow their minor children to travel to or from transit countries alone or with someone else.
- Transit countries authorities have sometimes blocked U.S. citizen minors from leaving when traveling without a parent or guardian.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information.html).
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html).
