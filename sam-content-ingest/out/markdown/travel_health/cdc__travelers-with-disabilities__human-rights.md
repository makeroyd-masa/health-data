# Travelers with Disabilities — Human rights

Each country has its own standard of accessibility for people with disabilities. Unlike the United States, many countries do not legally require accommodations for people with disabilities. Several websites can help the traveler answer questions about accessibility for a specific destination or provide support if an emergency occurs. Travel agents, hotels, airlines, or cruise ship companies can also serve as sources for information about services available during the trip and at the destination, including for [service animals](/yellow-book/hcp/family-travel/traveling-with-pets-and-service-animals.html). Table 2.3.2 includes resources for travelers with disabilities to help them gather information about accommodations and human rights frameworks at their destination.
