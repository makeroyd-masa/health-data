#### Traveling with children

- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction.](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html) You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information.html) in Switzerland.
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html).

#### Traveling through Europe

If you are planning to visit or travel through European countries, you should be familiar with the requirements of the [Schengen Agreement](/en/international-travel/planning/guidance/europe.html).

- Your passport should be valid for at least three months beyond the period of stay if you plan on transiting a Schengen country. Review our [U.S. Travelers in Europe page](/en/international-travel/planning/guidance/europe.html) for more information.
- You will need sufficient proof of funds and a return plane ticket.
- For additional information about visas for the Schengen area, see the [Schengen Visa page](https://schengenvisainfo.com/).

---

#### Entry and exit procedures

You can enter the Schengen area for up to 90 days without a visa for tourism or business purposes. Visit the website of the [Embassy of Liechtenstein](https://www.liechtensteinusa.org/) in Washington, D.C. for the most current visa information.

You must have the appropriate visa if you are traveling to Liechtenstein for the following reasons:

- Employment
- Study
- Residency

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of Liechtenstein.

---

#### Currency

- The Swiss Franc (CHF) is the official currency of Liechtenstein.
- Most major credit cards are widely accepted, but some vendors will only accept chip-and-PIN cards.
- ATMs are widely available and accept U.S. debit cards.
- Numerous banks do not accept U.S. citizens as clients.
- Review the [Embassy’s website](https://ch.usembassy.gov/banking-resources/?_ga=2.88418130.892137477.1758807142-707128581.1732185470) for more information on banking.
