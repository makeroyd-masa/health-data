# Indonesia — IndonesiaTraveler View

#### Flooding

Several Indonesian islands are experiencing major flooding. Travel by land may be dangerous in flood zones and the healthcare infrastructure has been damaged in these areas. There may be an increased risk of waterborne, vector-borne, and fungal diseases. [Avoid contact with floodwater.](https://www.cdc.gov/floods/safety/floodwater-after-a-disaster-or-emergency-safety.html) Follow all instructions from local authorities.

#### Rabies

Increased cases of [rabies](https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/index.html) have been reported in humans in West Timor, Indonesia. To [prevent rabies](https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/prevention/index.html#:~:text=deaths%20each%20year.-,Prevention,-The%20best%20way), travelers should avoid wild animals and unfamiliar dogs and cats. Before traveling abroad, consult your healthcare provider about your risk of exposure to rabies. [Pre-exposure vaccination](https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/hcp/prevention-recommendations/pre-exposure-prophylaxis.html) may be recommended. If bitten, scratched, or licked by a wild or unfamiliar animal during travel, seek immediate medical attention. Rabies is fatal if it’s untreated before symptoms start.
