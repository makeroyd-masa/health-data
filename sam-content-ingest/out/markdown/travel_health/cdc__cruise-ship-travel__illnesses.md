# Cruise Ship Travel — Illnesses

For public health responses to ill cruise ship passengers and crew, control measures typically are initiated while the ship is still at sea. CDC port health station personnel obtain clinical and epidemiologic information about the ill or deceased person(s), determine public health risk, and provide guidance to the ship's healthcare professionals about case findings, infection control measures, and contact investigations.

CDC port health station personnel might respond by meeting a ship at the port of entry to further investigate or to assist the responding health department with surveillance and control measures. CDC personnel also might provide onsite response for outbreaks or clusters of disease, quarantinable communicable diseases, and some vaccine-preventable diseases (e.g., measles, rubella). CDC's [Vessel Sanitation Program](/vessel-sanitation/about/) is responsible for responding to reports of acute gastroenteritis on cruise ships. CDC's Legionella Team is responsible for responding to reports of legionellosis.

CDC provides guidance to cruise lines on managing ill travelers at [Cruise Ship Guidance: Disease-Specific Management of Ill Passengers/Crew](/port-health/php/maritime-guidance/index.html).
