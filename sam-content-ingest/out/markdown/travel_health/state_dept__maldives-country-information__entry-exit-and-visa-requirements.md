# Maldives Country Information — Entry, exit, and visa requirements

#### Helpful resources

- [Embassy of the Maldives in the United States](https://us.mdvmission.gov.mv/index.php)
- To extend your visa visit the [Department of Immigration and Emigration](https://www.immigration.gov.mv/).
- To submit your traveler declaration, visit the [IMUGA site](https://imuga.immigration.gov.mv/traveller).

---

#### Entry requirements

The following documents are required for entry:

- A valid passport.
- Onward or return ticket.
- Sufficient funds.
- A no-cost visitor visa valid for 30 days is issued upon arrival.
- All travelers to and from the Maldives must fill out and submit a [Traveler Declaration](https://imuga.immigration.gov.mv/traveller). You must complete this within 96 hours before travel and you may submit the form electronically. Travelers should only submit their information via the government [IMUGA site](https://imuga.immigration.gov.mv/) ([IMUGA)](https://imuga.immigration.gov.mv/)
- Watch out for non-government sites claiming to offer visa services for a fee. These sites are scams. Do not trust them.

---

#### Extended stays

- The [Department of Immigration and Emigration](https://www.immigration.gov.mv/) often approves stay extensions up to 90 days. Travelers need to show they have enough money to stay. They must also stay in a resort, hotel, or provide a letter from a local sponsor.
- Anyone staying more than 60 days without proper authorization faces heavy fines of 15,000 MVR or deportation.

---

#### Traveling with children

- Always bring a copy of each child’s birth certificate or other evidence of your legal relationship to each child.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information.html) in the Maldives.
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html).

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of the Maldives.

---

#### Currency

The Maldivian Rufiyaa is the official currency of the Maldives.

- U.S. Dollars are widely accepted and easily exchanged for local currency.
- ATMs are available in tourist areas but may be scarce in remote locations.
- U.S. Dollar withdrawals are only possible with local bank cards.
