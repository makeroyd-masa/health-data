# Chile Country Information — Entry, exit, and visa requirements

#### Helpful resources

- [Embassy of Chile in the United States](https://www.chile.gob.cl/estados-unidos/en "Embassy of Chile in the United States").
- Review our information on [Dual Nationality](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/dual-nationality.html "Dual Nationality").

---

#### Traveling with children

Always bring a copy of each child’s birth certificate or other evidence of your legal relationship to each child.

- When 1 parent is traveling with a child, a notarized consent letter is required from the absent parent. Otherwise, you must show proof of sole custody.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html "Abduction Prevention"). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](/en/intercountry-parental-child-abduction.html "Intercountry Parental Child Abduction") in Chile.
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html "Traveling with Minors").

##### **Required documents for minors**

Minors leaving Chile must show Chilean airport officials the following at the time of departure:

- An original notarized permission letter from any parent not traveling with them, along with a photocopy
- An original apostilled birth certificate
- If the non-traveling parent is in Chile, get the letter notarized by a Chilean notary.
- If the non-traveling parent is in the United States, get the letter notarized at the [Chilean Embassy or Chilean Consulate](https://www.chile.gob.cl/chile/ "Chilean Embassy or Chilean Consulate").
- If a U.S. notary signs the letter, it must be apostilled to be valid in Chile. Get the apostille well before your trip. The process can take a long time.

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

- The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of Chile.

---

#### Currency

- The Chilean Peso (CLP$) is the official currency of Chile.
- Credit cards are widely used across Chile.
- Chile has ATMs that accept international and U.S.-issued bankcards.
