#### Traveling with children

- Always bring a copy of each child’s birth certificate or other evidence of your legal relationship to each child.
- When one parent is traveling with a child, a notarized consent letter is required from the absent parent. Otherwise, you must show proof of sole custody.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on how to [prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information/Comoros.html) in Comoros.
- Review our information on [Traveling with Minors](/en/international-travel/planning/personal-needs/minors.html).

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents of Comoros.

---

#### Currency

- The Comorian Franc (KMF) is the official currency of Comoros.
- Visas on arrival must be purchased with either U.S. dollars or Euros. Visas on arrival cost between 30 and 50 Euros.
- Comoros mostly uses cash. Credit cards are not widely accepted.
- Euros are often accepted for purchases with change given in KMF.
- Mobile money is commonly used in Comoros.
