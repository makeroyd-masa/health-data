# Uganda — UgandaTraveler View

CDC has temporarily restricted entry for certain travelers who were recently in DRC, South Sudan, or Uganda. Travelers should follow [after travel](https://www.cdc.gov/viral-hemorrhagic-fevers/after-travel/index.html) recommendations until 21 days after leaving an affected country.

The Ugandan Ministry of Health has reported an outbreak of [Marburg Virus Disease (MVD)](https://www.cdc.gov/marburg/index.html) in Kyegegwa District, Uganda. If you travel to this district, you should avoid contact with people who are sick and their blood or body fluids or contaminated objects. You should also avoid contact with bats and non-human primates. If you develop [symptoms](https://www.cdc.gov/marburg/about/index.html#cdc_disease_basics_symptoms-symptoms) while in Kyegegwa District or within 21 days after leaving, isolate immediately, do not travel, and contact local health authorities.
