# Angola Country Information — Travel requirements

- You may visit Angola visa-free as a tourist for up to 30 days per trip (90 days total per calendar year).
- A visa is required for a visit of more than 30 days.
- If you require a visa, apply as soon as possible. The wait time may be long.
- Review visa requirements at the nearest Angolan embassy or consulate or at the [Angolan Migration Service](http://www.smevisa.gov.ao/ "Angolan Migration Visa Service") website for visas and visa pre-approval.
- [Serviço de Migração e Estrangeiros](http://www.smevisa.gov.ao/ "Serviço de Migração e Estrangeiros")

Travelers are required to show proof of yellow fever vaccination when traveling from or via countries with risk for yellow fever transmission. U.S. travelers may be asked to show proof of yellow fever vaccination when requesting a visa or attempting to enter Angola, regardless of their country of departure.

Stay up to date with the vaccines recommended by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention:

- [CDC Travelers’ Health](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/destinations/traveler/none/angola?s_cid=ncezid-dgmq-travel-single-001)
- [World Health Organization Travel Advice](https://www.who.int/travel-advice)

- Your passport must be valid for the duration of your stay.
- 1 blank page required for entry.

- Entry maximum: $10,000 or equivalent.
- Exit maximum: $10,000 or equivalent.
- Upon entry or exit, any amount over the limit may be seized. Limits may change.
- Verify with the [Embassy of Angola in Washington, DC](https://angola.org/visa-requirements/) or the [National Bank.](https://angola.org/visa-requirements/)
