#### Traveling with children

- Sierra Leonean authorities have sometimes blocked U.S. citizen minors from leaving when traveling without a parent or guardian.
- Parents and guardians should prepare a notarized, written permission letter to allow their minor children to travel to or from Sierra Leone alone, with only 1 parent, or with someone else who is not the parent.
- If you have concerns that your child may be abducted abroad by their other parent or guardian, review our information on [how to prevent an international parental child abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/prevention.html). You can also review our [information on Parental Child Abduction](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/International-Parental-Child-Abduction/International-Parental-Child-Abduction-Country-Information.html) in Sierra Leone.
- Review our information on Traveling with Minors.

---

#### HIV and AIDS restrictions

The U.S. Department of State is unaware of any HIV and AIDS entry restrictions for visitors to or foreign residents living in Sierra Leone.

---

#### Currency

- The New Leone (Nle) is the official currency of Sierra Leone.
- Only a few establishments accept credit cards, sometimes requiring a large additional fee. Be prepared to pay bills in cash.
- Freetown has ATMs that accept U.S.-issued bankcards. But ATMs are frequently out of order and may charge high fees.
