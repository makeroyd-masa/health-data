# Philippines — PhilippinesTraveler View

The Philippines Food and Drug Administration has reported the circulation of counterfeit [Speeda human rabies vaccine](https://www.fda.gov.ph/fda-advisory-no-2025-0853-public-health-warning-against-the-purchase-and-use-of-the-counterfeit-drug-product-purified-rabies-vaccine-vero-cell-speeda-2-5-i-u-freeze-dried-powder-for-i/) and [Equirab rabies antiserum](https://www.fda.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/FDA-ADVISORY-NO.-2025-1642-A-1.pdf). These products may not be effective in the prevention of rabies and could contain harmful ingredients.

To [prevent rabies](https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/prevention/index.html#:~:text=deaths%20each%20year.-,Prevention,-The%20best%20way), travelers should avoid wild animals and unfamiliar dogs and cats. Before traveling abroad, consult your healthcare provider about your risk of exposure to rabies. [Pre-exposure vaccination](https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/hcp/clinical-care/pre-exposure-prophylaxis.html) may be recommended. If bitten, scratched, or licked by a wild or unfamiliar animal during travel, seek immediate medical attention. Rabies is fatal if it’s untreated before symptoms start.
