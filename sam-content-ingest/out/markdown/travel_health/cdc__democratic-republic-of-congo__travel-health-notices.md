# Democratic Republic Of Congo — Travel Health Notices

Be aware of current health issues in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Learn how to protect yourself.

#### Level 3 Reconsider Nonessential Travel

- Updated
  [Ebola Bundibugyo Virus Disease in Parts of the Democratic Republic of the Congo](/travel/notices/level3/ebola-democratic-republic-of-the-congo)
  June 15, 2026
  The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) is experiencing an outbreak of Bundibugyo virus disease (BVD) in Ituri, Nord-Kivu (North Kivu), and Sud-Kivu (South Kivu) provinces. CDC recommends avoiding nonessential travel to these provinces.

#### Level 2 Practice Enhanced Precautions

- Updated
  [Ebola Bundibugyo Virus Disease in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda](/travel/notices/level2/ebola-drc-uganda)
  June 15, 2026
  The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) and Uganda are experiencing outbreaks of Bundibugyo virus disease (BVD). CDC recommends practicing enhanced precautions for travel to Uganda or to provinces in the DRC other than those affected by the outbreak.
- [Meningococcal Disease in the Democratic Republic of the Congo](/travel/notices/level2/meningococcal-drc)
  March 24, 2026
  There is an outbreak of meningococcal disease in the Mangembo Health Zone of Kongo Central Province. The best way to protect yourself is to get meningococcal vaccination.
- [Global Polio](/travel/notices/level2/global-polio)
  March 09, 2026
  Some international destinations have circulating poliovirus. Before any international travel, make sure you are up to date on your polio vaccines.

#### Level 1 Practice Usual Precautions

- [Global Measles](/travel/notices/level1/measles-globe)
  May 28, 2025
  Measles cases are rising in many countries around the world. All international travelers should be fully vaccinated against measles with the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine, according to CDC's measles vaccination recommendations for international travel.
